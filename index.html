Overenrollment at Middlebury College.
Before the pandemic (pre-spring 2020), in any given semester, there were typically about 2,580 students studying on campus at Middlebury College.

1 stick-figure = 50 students.

We also show the 735 first year students, broken down by 628 'regular' fall and 107 'february' students.

These numbers are pulled from 2018-2019's first-year population. The regular fall 2018 class size (excluding febs) represents roughly 25% of the total student population on campus at that time.
Here's a box to hold all those people. That box is the Middlebury College campus.
In non-pandemic circumstances, roughly 300 students are typically abroad. These students are not counted in the enrollment, and, are obviously not on campus.

Fast forward to Spring of 2021, a year after settling into the pandemic.

There are 1,998 students studying on campus and 436 students studying remotely.


This spring of 2021:

  • 26 students are abroad (compared to 300 on a normal year)
  • 323 students are on leave of absence and will return in Fall 2021
  • 75 students deferred from the class of 2024 to 2025

None of these 860 students are physically on campus.
The box represents the 2,580 students that Middlebury was used to accomodating in a pre-pandemic semester.
And here's the 2,858 students that are on campus this fall.
This fall, there are 680 first-year 'regular' students (excluding febs) -- this represents only about 50 more first-years than during a typical year. Additionally, this is still roughly 25% of the total student population currently on campus, which is what we might typically expect.

You may also recognize the 323 who took a leave of abscence, and the 75 first-year students who had deferred.
